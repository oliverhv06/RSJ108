How to Take Photos on a Digital Camera

Task time: ~10-15 minutes
Awesome rating: As awesome as your photos!

Are you trying to take your stories to the next level? Photos are a great way to do that! Learning how to take your own is a great way to improve your storytelling skills and get exactly what you need!

  • Things You'll Need

  • Digital Camera
  • Laptop
  • (SD) Memory Card
  • USB-A to USB-C Cord

  1. Turn the camera on.

    Start with simply turning your camera on. Most cameras have a small on button on the side or a switch/toggle at the top. If you can't find where these are, consult the manufacturer's instruction, which should explain how to find and use the button/switch/toggle/etc. Note: Digital cameras are battery operated! If the camera is not turning on, it could be because the battery is dead. Most cameras come with a charger that plugs into the wall.

  2. Insert a memory card.

    More recent digital camera models may not let you take any photos without a memory card. You can buy them at electronics stores or some department stores like Walmart. The most common type of memory card is Secure Digital (SD). Digital cameras often have the slot to hold memory cards on the side or bottom of the camera. Sometimes this is the same compartment as the battery. If you cannot find where to insert your memory card or don't know how, consult the manufacturer's manual.

    Someone putting a SD memory card into a digital camera.

  3. Familiarize yourself with the buttons.

    Digital cameras have tons of buttons. Taking time to get to learn each of them will really help you get the best photos possible. Some of them are used to adjust zoom, lighting, or brightness. This 5 minute tutorial goes through what some of the buttons are on a camera model similar to the Z50 photo cameras available in the RSJ equipment checkout lab!

  4. Take a few pictures.

    This is the fun part! Choose a subject like a pet, landscape, or a friend and take a moment to focus your camera. You can adjust focus with buttons or dials depending on your model and check how it looks with the camera's monitor. Then, when you're satisfied with how it looks, press the shutter button and your camera should capture the image. Voila!

  5. View your pictures.

    Once you've taken a few pictures, you should go back to see how they turned out. To do this, press on the play button. The pictures you took should appear on the camera's monitor and you can scroll through them using the scroll wheel. Don't like what you see? You can press the delete button and the photo will be removed from your memory card.

    Someone reviewing a photo they took of a woman with their digital camera monitor.

  6. Transfer your photos to your computer.

    Once you have a few photos you like, you'll want to transfer them onto a computer or laptop. This saves space on your memory card while keeping access and allowing you to edit them. The process is usually fairly simple, but if you're confused, you can refer to your instruction manual. For most modern cameras, you would need a USB-A to USB-C cord, with the C end going the camera and A end going into your laptop. Once plugged in, a folder should appear on your laptop for you to upload the photos onto it.
